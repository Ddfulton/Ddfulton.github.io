    Société Générale, often called SocGen is a French multinational investment bank and financial services company headquartered in Paris. The company is a universal bank and counts the divisions French Networks, Global Transaction Banking, International Retail Banking, Financial Services, Corporate and Investment Banking, Private Banking, Asset Management and Securities Services among their services.

    SocGen is France's third largest bank by total assets after BNP Paribas and Crédit Agricole. It also the sixth largest bank in Europe and the world's eighteenth. The company is a component of the Euro Stoxx 50 stock market index. It is considered a systemically important bank by the Financial Stability Board. It is known as one of the Trois Vieilles ("Old Three") of French banking, along with BNP Paribas and Crédit Lyonnais.

    Operating Income by department

    The Global Banking and Investor Solutions divison reported the largest operating income in 2020 of $8.692B, followed by International Retail Banking and Financial Services with $8.590B, French Retail Banking with $8.352B and Corporate Center with $-387M.

    Operating Income by region

    SocGen reports its largest operating income in its home market France with $11.174B. In the Rest of Europe it generates a total operating income of $8.810B. Africa, America and Asia/Oceania are roughly equally distributed with $1.866B, $1.812B and $1.584B, respectively.

    Net Income before taxes by department

    Net Income before taxes in 2020 was strongest by International Retail Banking and Financial Services by $2.434B followed by French Retail Banking by $1.092B and negative for Global Banking and Investor Solutions by $-20M and for Corporate Center by $1.907B.

    BNP Paribas is a French multinational banking group. It is the largest bank in Europe and the seventh largest bank in the world by total assets. The bank was formed through the merger of Banque Nationale de Paris (BNP) and Paribas in 2000. It's corporate identity descends from its foundation in 1848 as a French national bank. Today, it belongs to one of the four major French international banks alongside Crédit Agricole, Société Générale and Groupe BPCE. The group is listed on the first market of Euronext Paris and a component of the Euro Stoxx 50 stock market index, while it also included in the French CAC 40 index.

    BNP Paribas provides a range of banking and financial services in France and internationally. It operates through two divisions, Retail Banking and Services, and Corporate and Institutional Banking. Its retail banking networks serving more than 30 million customers in three domestic markets, France, Italy, Belgium and Luxembourg, through retail brands, BNP Paribas, BNL and Fortis respectively. BNP Paribas also has retail subsidiaries operating in Poland, Turkey, Ukraine and northern Africa. In the Americas, it operates in western Midwest and western United States as Bank of the West. As an investment bank and international financial services provider for corporate and institutional clients, the group is present across Europe, the Americas, and Asia.

    Operating Income by department

    The International Financial Services divison reported the largest operating income in 2020 of $18.196B, followed by Domestic Markets with $17.049B, Corporate & Institutional Banking with $15.714B and Other Activities with $-408M.

    Operating Income by region

    BNP Paribas reports its largest operating income in Europe with $36.488B. In North America, it generates a total operating income of $6.529B. In Asia & Pacific the total operating income that was generated amounts to $4.337B and in other regions, $3.197B.

    Net Income before taxes by department

    Net Income before taxes in 2020 was strongest for Corporate & Institutional Banking by $3.943B, followed by International Financial Services at $3.906B and Domestic Markets with $3.738B and negative for Other Activities by $-373M.

    Banco Santander is a Spanish multinational bank based in Madrid and Santander in Spain. The company was formerly known as Banco Santander Central Hispano S.A. and changed its name to Banco Santander, S.A. in June 2007. It is present in all global financial centres as the 16th-largest banking instition.

    Banco Santander provides various retail and commercial banking products and services to individuals, small and medium-sized enterprises, and large companies worldwide. It offers demand and time deposits, and current and savings accounts; mortgages, consumer finance, syndicated corporate loans, structured financing, cash management, export and agency finance, trade and working capital solutions, and corporate finance; and insurance products. The company also provides cash, asset, and wealth management; and private banking services. In addition, it is involved in the corporate banking; treasury, risk hedging, foreign trade, confirming, custody, and investment banking activities. The company operates through a network of 11,236 branches.

    Operating Income by department

    The Retail banking divison reported the largest operating income in 2020 of $43.413B, followed by Corporate & Investment Banking with $6.089B, Wealth Management & Insurance with $2.319B and PagoNxt with $407M.

    Operating Income by region

    Santander reports its largest operating income in South America by $16.977B closely followed with $16.754B in Europe. In North America, it generates a total operating income of $12.598B and its Digital Consumer bank generates $5.898B in operating income.

    Net Income before taxes by department

    Net Income before taxes in 2020 was strongest for Retail Banking by $8.982B, followed by Corporate & Investment Banking at $3.070B and Wealth Management & Insurance with $1.292B and negative for PagoNxt by $-115M.

    Crédit Agricole Group, sometimes called La banque verte (the green bank due to its historical ties to farming, is a French multinational financial services group and the world's largest cooperative financial institution. It is the second largest bank in France, after BNP Paribas, the third largest in Europe and the tenth largest in the world. It is headquarted in Montrouge, France.

    The bank provides retail, corporate, insurance, and investment banking products and services worldwide. It operates through five segments: Asset Gathering; French Retail Banking - LCL; International Retail Banking; Specialised Financial Services; and Large Customers. Crédit Agricole S.A. serves retail and corporate customers, banks and financial institutions, government agencies, and local authorities.

    Operating Income by business line

    The Large customer divison reported the largest operating income in 2020 of $7.189B, followed by Asset gathering with $6.548B, French retail banking with $4.020B, International retail banking with $3.036B and Specialised financial services with $2.884B.

    Operating Income by region

    Crédit Agricole reports its largest operating income in its home market France by $12.432B, followed with $4.502B in other European countries (ex. Italy) and $3.541B in Italy. In North America, it generates a total operating income of $1.267B, in Asia Pacific (ex. Japan) $673M, in Africa $537M, in Japan $333M and in Central and South America $118M.

    Net Income before taxes by business

    Net Income before taxes in 2020 was strongest for Asset gathering with $3.286B, followed by Large customers with $1.933B and Specialised financial services with $967M. It was lowest for French retail banking with $929M and almost half of it for International retail banking with $465M.

    ING is a Dutch international banking group operating in 40+ countries with its core base in Europe. It is headquarted in Amsterdam and has more than 57,000 employees serving 53.2M customers, corporate clients and financial institutions (by 2020). It is one of the top 30 largest bank globally and counts to the ten largest banks in Europe by revenue.

    Their products range from savings, payments, investments, loans and mortgages in most of their retail markets. For Wholesale Banking clients, ING provides specialised lending, tailored corporate finance, debt and equity market solutions, sustainable finance solutions, payments & cash management and trade and treasury services.
