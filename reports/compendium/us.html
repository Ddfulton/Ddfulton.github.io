    JPMorgan Chase & Co. is a multinational investment bank and financial services holding company headquartered in New York City. The bank is incorporated in Delaware. As of June 30, 2021, it is the largest bank in the United States and the fifth-largest bank in the world in terms of total assets, with total assets of US$3.684 trillion.

    As a "Bulge Bracket" bank, JPMorgan Chase is a major provider of various investment banking and financial services. As of 2021 it is the largest lender to the fossil fuel industry in the world which is obviously not attractive to ESG-motivated investors.

    Along with Bank of America, Citigroup and Wells Fargo, JPMorgan Chase belongs to America's "Big Four" banks. JPMorgan Chase is a universal bank as well as a custodian bank. The J.P. Morgan brand is used by the investment banking, asset management, private banking, private wealth management, and treasury services divisions. Fiduciary activity within private banking and private wealth management is done under the aegis of JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A.—the actual trustee. The Chase brand is used for credit card services in the United States and Canada, the bank's retail banking activities in the United States, and commercial banking.

    Wells Fargo is an American multinational financial services company headquartered in San Francisco and New York City. The company operates in 35 countries with over 70M clients. Along with JP Morgan Chase, Bank of America and Citigroup, Wells Fargo counts to the "Big Four of Banks" of the United States. It is the fourth largest bank in US by total assets and is one of the largest as ranked by bank deposits and market capitalization. It has 8050 branches and 13000 ATMs and is one of the most valuable bank brands.

    The company operates through various different segements, including consumer banking and lending, commercial banking, corporate and investment banking, wealth and investment banking, securities, loans, deposits as well as regulation and supervision.
