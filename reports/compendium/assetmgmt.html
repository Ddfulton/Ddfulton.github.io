    Robinhood operates a financial services platform in the United States. Its platform allows users to invest in stocks, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), options, gold, and cryptocurrencies. The company also offers various learning and education solutions comprise Snacks, a digest of business news stories; Learn, which is a collection of approximately 650 articles, including guides, feature tutorials, and financial dictionary; Newsfeeds that offer access to free premium news from various sites, such as Barron’s, Reuters, and The Wall Street Journal; and lists, which allow users to create custom watchlists to monitor securities, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies, as well as cash management services. Robinhood Markets, Inc. was incorporated in 2013 and is headquartered in Menlo Park, California.

    The company currently has 18M active users with $80B in assets. Most of its customers are Millenials entering the stock market during the pandemic.

    Investing with Robinhood is commission-free. The company generates money through payment for order flow, premium membership fees, stock loans, interest on uninvested cash, interchange fees related to its debit card, and other smaller revenue streams.

    Marcus is Goldman Sachs' first foray into retail. They offer savings, consumer lending and robo-advising. Along with Ally Bank they are the only ones to offer any interest to consumers, at 0.50% APY for US customers. Goldman has not reinvented the wheel with Marcus but they have packaged it into a simple yet powerful UI and backed it with their strong goodwill. As of now they operate in the US and the UK.

    As of January 2021 Marcus had already attracted $97B of deposits, providing a nicely diversified source of retail deposits (though at the steep cost of 50bps).

    Trade Republic Bank GmbH operates as a mobile and commission-free broker that enables users to trade stocks, derivatives, and ETFs on the stock exchange. The company offers commission-free trading, monitored stock exchange price, and depot services. It also provides a real-time data application that gives users with real-time data, price alerts, and notifications. The company was founded in 2015 and is based in Berlin, Germany. The company has grown to more than 1M users, 400 employees and €6B assets under management. The last funding round makes Trade Republic one of the most valuable fintech start-ups in Europe. The company’s core markets are Germany, France and Austria but it plans to roll out its services across all eurozone countries in the next 12 months. It obtained its full banking license by the BaFin in 2019, making it a legitimate and recognized securities trading bank.

    Trade Republic receives earnings from its partners such as Blackrock or HSBC in the form of commissions - for every transaction or purchase of a security. The reimbursement is “up to” €3 per order. Refunds are common as other online brokers also receive them from their partners. Trade Republic currently counts four cooperation partners - Lang & Schwarz Exchange, HSBC, Blackrock and Solarisbank. Also, Trade Republic receives a €1 fee from its customers for every trade, regardless of the trade volume. This is an advantage for Trade Republic, as most customers are individuals making small, frequent trades, so this adds up. In addition, Trade Republic does not charge any deposit, withdrawal or inactivity fees. This is partly made possible by the fact that Trade Republic is a legal banking entity connected to all the other national banks in Germany.

    One drawback of Trade Republic is that it’s only available through a mobile app, which means that web and desktop trading are off the table. For some traders, this might not be an issue, but for professional traders who rely on their ability to customize their setup to maximize their profits, they might not feel comfortable allocating thousands of euros from their mobile phone.
