One of the strategic changes that was beginning to occur before the outbreak of Covid was the emergence of more partnerships between fintechs, other technology companies and banks. Many start-ups realised that banks offer complementary resources that they need in order to make their business models work. These include banking licenses, regulatory know-how and existing customer bases. In the disruptive phase start-ups thought they could circumvent these resources whilst banks didnâ€™t think they needed to offer the solutions the start-ups provided. Since then, both groups, pressured by cash burn on the one side and strategic optionality on the other, have begun to see the benefits of partnerships. The Covid situation is creating new urgency to accelerate that development.

There are four types of partnerships incumbents can engage in with Fintechs

Cooperation: Partnerships, cooperations or joint ventures with Fintechs to develop new technologies and products/services. As well as cooperations with Fintechs to use their products.

Funding support: Development of Accelerator programs, tech-hackathons, support through mentoring programs. The focus of these sponsorships is mainly on non-financial support.

Investments: Investment of equity in Fintechs - mainly minority shareholdings. Investement can take the form of dedicated investment vehicles, independent funds or participation in financing rounds.

M&A: Complete acquisition of a Fintech. Post-merger integration into the buyer's identity, including integration of the product range into the product portfolio of the buyer bank.
